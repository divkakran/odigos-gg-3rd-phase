ANNEXURE 2

Terms and conditions regulating the conduct and performance of licensed Tourist Guides

  • The licensed guide, when on duty, shall; always wear the laminated guide license issued by Ministry of Tourism and also wear the name tag while accompanying tourists.
  • The licensed guide would not lend his / her guide license or any other document which may have been issued to him / her by Ministry of Tourism to any other person(s).
  • In case of loss or damage to the guide license the guide would immediately inform the office of concerned India Tourism and the nearest Police Station. The Guide would have to apply afresh for the duplicate guide license on the basis of First Information Report (FIR) and statement that the guide license has been lost. The guide would be responsible for the safe custody of the guide License.
  • The guide would not solicit tips from tourists.
  • The licensed guide would dress in a presentable manner to uphold the dignity and honor of the profession to which he / she belongs and of the country.
  • The guides would charge the guide fees which should be in compliance with fees fixed from time to time by Tourist Guides Federation of India in consultation with Indian Association of Tour Operators IATO / TAAI representatives.
  • The guide would not canvass business of any kind on behalf of the business houses, travel agents, hoteliers, paying - guest houses, shopkeepers, transport operators, etc., nor he / she would accept monetary commission from them or indulge in any other unethical practices.
  • The guide would not enter into any other business arrangements with any of the establishments either by way of partnership or on commission basis for canvassing business of any kind.
  • Guides found to be canvassing for shopping establishments like shops selling handicrafts etc., could be complained and liable for deactivation along with penalty.
  • The guide would take tourists only for sightseeing to the monuments and public buildings, which are a part of the sightseeing tour included in the itinerary of the tourists. Under no circumstances should any guide accompany the tourists to shops. In case, any guide is found to have contravened this provision he/she may be reported and his / her registration is liable to be cancelled / revoked or penalised for the amount of his/her Guide Fee.
  • The guide would not refuse without valid reasons any assignments given to him / her and if the guide repeatedly refuses three assignments in a season, his / her registration would be liable to be suspended / revoked.
  • The guide would maintain good conduct and courteous behavior towards tourists or other officials with whom he / she comes in contact during the course of his / her assignment.
  • The guide would be liable to following penalties in case of late arrival for assignments:-
    • First late arrival: warning in writing.
    • Second late arrival: deduction of 25% of fees.
    • Third late arrival: deactivation for one month when the second and third late arrivals are both within a period of six months.
    • Total absence: deactivation for a period of three months.
      Note: The above penalties would apply to a guide who after having accepted an assignment does not report for duty at the appointed time except in such cases where late arrival / absence were due to circumstances beyond his / her control. Complaints regarding habitual late arrivals for assignments, etc., would be viewed seriously and can lead to deactivation and termination.
  • The guide would be required to submit a medical certificate of fitness on registration and thereafter, whenever required by Birdres
  • Absence of more than three months from the guiding profession must be reported in writing.
  • The registration of a guide who is arrested by the Police for offence of misconduct involving moral turpitude shall be withdrawn during the pendency of the case against him / her. In the event of his conviction by a Court of Law, the agreement shall be terminated and registration revoked and cancelled.
  • The guide would be responsible for warning foreign tourists against taking photographs of places which are prohibited by law or which may reflect adversely on the country's image.
  • The guide would accept assignments only to the region for which a guiding license has been granted by the India Tourism Office. He would be entitled to accompany tour groups on all - India basis as an Escort on payment of fees by the travel agents to be mutually negotiated. On Escort duty, a licensed guide would only conduct sight - seeing of monuments under the area of jurisdiction which would be spelt out in his / her guide license. At all other places, the services of licensed guides of that relevant region / area shall have to be engaged.
  • If a guide remains absent from active guiding for a period exceeding two years due to reasons of health, absence from country, etc., he / she shall be deemed to have left the profession and in that event the registration shall stand cancelled. However, in case a guide wants to re-register after an absence of two years he / she would have undergone a Refresher Course conducted by designated Institute in coordination with the Ministry of Tourism.
  • The guide would abide by the existing rules and any other rules and conditions that may be framed from time to time by the Ministry of Tourism and the Archaeological survey of India, failing which the registration shall be revoked and cancelled.
  • The Guide shall abide by the provisions of Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites & Remains Act (AM & ASR) Act 1958 and Rules 1959.
  • The Guide shall abide to the instructions given by site in charge or field staff while taking round in the ASI Monument premises.
  • The guide shall make taking entry in the register kept at ASI monument while taking visitors to that particular monument.
  • The Guide shall abide by the Code of Conduct for Safe and Honourable Tourism as adopted by the Ministry of Tourism and at all times follow the provisions of the code. (available at http://tourism.gov.in/sites/default/files/060320111104524_0.pdf)
